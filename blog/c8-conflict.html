Conflict

February 15, 2015

A common situation where conflicts will occur is when you are living with other people. I have had many conflicts with roommates and the most common source of this conflict is usually in the kitchen. The kitchen is neutral shared ground and the kitchen sink tends to get overpopulated with dirty dishes.

I will admit that sometimes I was the sink clogging culprit. One of my roommates would sometimes yell at me or leave me passive aggressive notes about my dirty dishes. This would anger me and would cause me to go into defensive mode. In return I would not do the dishes, which really felt like the only option. This didnâ€™t solve the conflict, and eventually became one of the underlining causes of us not living together anymore.

If I could go back I would tell the person that yelling and being aggressive is not the way to get positive reactions from people. If they have a problem with me they can just ask me in a civil way to alter my behavior.Ironically I am now much better at taking care of my dirty dishes. This experience really taught me that being aggressive towards someone is not the way to get their cooperation. Being right is not justification for aggressive action.
