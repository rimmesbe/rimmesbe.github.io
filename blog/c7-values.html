Self-Affirmation: Locking onto personal values

February 6, 2015

In my earlier blog I talked about Stereotype threat and how it can have negative effects on people’s performances. Luckily there are devices that can help combat this adverse effect. Self-affirmation can give a person the confidence and self-understanding to overcome Stereotype threat.

The values that come to mind when I reflect on the moments of happiness and satisfaction are:

Values: _______ Degree of Practice(1<--->5):
challenge-------------------4
ambition--------------------4
coolness under fire--------5
courage---------------------3
creativity-------------------4
excellence------------------4
growth----------------------5
influence-------------------3
knowledge-----------------5
personal development----4
quality----------------------5
will-power-----------------4
wisdom---------------------5

Coolness under fire to me means not panicking when a situation turns volatile. How you act and perform in those critical moments is the true measure of your ability at that point.

In my experience people mostly ask for advice about nontechnical, intangible, personal problems where direct answers or solutions just aren’t feasible. In these cases I usually find myself pushing people in the direction of growth, personal development, wisdom, and courage. People usually know the solution to their problems, it’s just difficult to summon the courage to make that decision (especially in the case of one’s own interests). Don’t do what’s easy, do what’s right.

I think my biggest challenge at DBC will not be defeating myself on an individual level, rather than on a stereotype level. I know there will be a mix of successes and failures, and I know there is no finish line. I think going on this journey with other people will be an invaluable support system.
